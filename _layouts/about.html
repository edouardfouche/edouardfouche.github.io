Short Biography

Edouard Fouché is a French-German expert in Data Science and has extensive experience in building efficient algorithms to address decision-making problems in energy systems. He is on a mission to make the energy grid better, faster, stronger. In 2016, he completed his studies in engineering and computer science at ESIEE Paris and the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT). In 2020, he graduated Summa Cum Laude with his Ph.D. in Data Science at KIT. He received the Helmholtz Doctoral Prize for his contributions to extracting knowledge from large data streams. After that, he became a group leader and the principal lecturer for Data Science at KIT. In 2021, Edouard obtained a MBA from the Quantic School of Business and Technology. He conducted a research stay at the University of Illinois in 2019 and the University of Kyoto in 2022 and published more than 15 research papers in top venues. His research was supported by the German Research Foundation (DFG), the Baden-Württemberg Foundation (BW-Stiftung), and the German Scholar Organization (GSO). In October 2023, he joined Siemens Smart Infrastructure as a Data Scientist for Product Innovation in Nürnberg, Germany.
